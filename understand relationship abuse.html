Understand Relationship Abuse

Physical violence isn't the only kind of abuse. To end the pain and stigma of domestic violence, you'll need a deeper awareness of the behaviours that constitute it, as well as examples of healthy relationships to guide your future decisions and interactions.

Abuse defined

Domestic violence (also known as intimate partner violence (IPV), dating abuse, or relationship abuse) is a set of behaviours employed by one partner in an intimate relationship to retain power and control over the other.

Domestic violence knows no bounds. Domestic abuse may affect people of any colour, age, gender, sexuality, religion, educational level, or socioeconomic background. Physical aggression, threats, emotional abuse, or financial control are all examples of behaviours that physically damage, frighten, manipulate, or control a spouse, or otherwise compel them to perform in ways they don't want to.
